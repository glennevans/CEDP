Special Needs

During my years of service, I have had the opportunity to work with a diverse group of students. Most special needs within my program have primarily been but not limited to mental health. One of the primary touch points when it comes to special needs is the IEP. There have been many students that have come across my path that needed an IEP, that did not request one, or were not aware that’s one of the services Student Services provides. In those cases where It appears the student could be struggling, making a recommendation to Student services is the first step.

One of the ways I accommodate my students is to provide multiple touchpoints for the class material. I provide extra time for assignments and tests if there is an IEP, all material is provided as written, and recorded documents. Every class I conduct is recorded for later viewing. The hope is that if there are any students who may have unidentified needs, can review the recording and ask questions at their own pace. An added benefit to the recordings, is live closed captioning for the hearing impaired. I am very fortunate to be teaching at an institution where our labs are all accessible. The only barrier that our program has in regards to special needs, is that there isn’t any accommodation in the visual arts for the visually impaired.
