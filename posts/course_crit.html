Course Outline

Motion and Interactive Design

 

Course Description:

This course expands on fundamental techniques and theories in Motion Design, emphasizing composition and storytelling with a focus on ideation from concept to completion of a narrative for advertising purposes. Students will explore the role of designers within the creative pipeline, through the creation and incorporation of storyboards, animatics, as well as 2D and 3D elements as they relate to time-based visuals. Content will focus on a range of creative, real world projects typically encountered within the graphic design industry.

 

CLO #1 – Analyze industry visual trends, in the design and execution of style frames, mood boards, and storyboards as they relate to project briefs for project pitch presentation.

 

EKS:
  • Examine current industry landscape as it relates to a project brief
  • Apply Design principals in the construction of style frames, and storyboards
  • Assess creative ideas in the design and construction of a mood boards, style frames, and storyboards
  • Demonstrate the creation of professional creative presentations as they relate to industry standards

 

CLO #2 – Apply 2D and 3D motion design techniques and practices within a digital composition according to industry standards.

 

EKS:
  • Interpret a client brief in the planning of a motion graphic display.
  • Execute treatment based on a specified script.
  • Export assets and media for delivery

 

CLO #3 – Employ motion tracking, rotoscoping and compositing in the creation of broadcast motion graphics as they relate to 2D and 3D motion graphics within the graphic design industry.

 

EKS:
  • Examination of creative briefs, what information to extract and action on.
  • Exam footage and plan proper usage of color correction and image stabilization.
  • Discuss the concept of motion tracking and roto within animations.
  • Export assets and media for delivery

 

CLO #4 – Create animations based on a specific script for digital display.

 

EKS:
  • Interpret a client brief in the planning of a motion graphic display.
  • Create a treatment proposal based on a specified script.
  • Create an animation that adheres to an approved plan.
  • Record and sync a script with an animation.

Course Critique

The design and multimedia field is one that moves very quickly. To that end, it is vital that courses in this discipline be reviewed and fine-tuned yearly. This year, by virtue of a cyclical review, I was able to lay the foundation to be able to create a course that will adapt and evolve as quickly as the industry and technology does.

One of the main issues with outlines from previous years, was the focus on specific technology and software. As the industry ebbs and flows, software changes at lightning speeds. As a result, the newly written outcomes and embedded knowledge skills focus on smaller learned tasks. These specific micro skills can then be transferred to larger more complex projects.
