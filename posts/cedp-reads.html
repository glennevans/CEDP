Future Learn

How to teach online was a learning module provided by Future learn. The Purpose of this course, was to help individuals level up their experience through targeted conversations, and classroom activities.

One of the biggest takeaways from the couse is the knowledge that we as educators are all in the same boat, and in this together. Many of the teachng stratagies and activities I have incorporated into my teaching.

When we first began teaching online, I felt that it was a huge undertaking. Trying to almost re-write my course delivery on the fly. Students weren't sure how to engage with the course, much less eachother. One of the teaching methods that was extracted from this course, was the Pecha Kucha presentation method. The idea being that you have 15 image slides to use for your presentation. After tyring this presentation method, found the students were more engaged with the class conversation, rather then just taking down notes.

LBGTQ+ Students in the college classroom

Language and words have weight. This is something that I came to realize after participating in this learning session. I have been fortunate enough to have had many LGBTQ+ friends and students in my life over the years. Through those years, i have taken many things for granted.

I used to believe that I was an ally to my friends and students, that I was able to think past their individual identification. People are people right? The main thing I caught myself doing quite often was to use the common colloquialism "Hey Guys". To me this was a simple greeting that I have used my entire life. I have used in with both male and femail collegues, and friend. Upon compleating this session, I took away a better understanding of how individuals wish do be addressed, as well as having a sensitivity to using language that is more gender neutral an inclusive.
