Engaging Students with Office 365

This approach will support students by leveraging software and technology that is already available, and easily accessible through the school LMS systems.

It will support the students by allowing greater connectivity to the class, and instructor; as well as timelier peer feedback and instructor evaluation.

Technology will consist of using three Office 365 tools in unison. Teams, SharePoint and OneNote.

Teams will be used for in-class communications. Groups have already been created for each class the student will be taking. Inside of Teams, each class has several discussion groups. The General room is where main class discussions and lectures can be held. This can be via text input, or live video chat.

A second group in the class is dedicated to class Q&A. Here general or specific questions relating to the course can be asked and answered. They can be answered by the instructor, or another student in the class that may be having the same issue. This Q&A is also moderated by the instructor.

Several other class groups are also created in the Teams environment for when group breakouts need to happen. Each one of these groups has the ability to be recorded and monitored by the instructor, and be reviewed by participants in that group.

An available add on to teams, is the ability to create a OneNote connection for the students. From here the instructor has the ability distribute material to each student individually, which then resides in their personal handouts folder.

For material that does not need to be submitted to the Black Board LMS – in class assignments can also be uploaded by the student. This in class work will reside within the students private OneNote folder. Only the instructor and the student have the ability to view its contents. This will allow for expedited feedback.

Student Feedback will be driven by using Microsoft Forms. These forms can quickly be integrated into the Class Teams channel, or a standalone URL. The forms are quick to create and the results are anonymous, and compiled quickly.

Lastly, a SharePoint site for the class will be created. Which will allow the students to see and search for class material in a more familiar context.
